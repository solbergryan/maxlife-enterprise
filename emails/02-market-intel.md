**The Central Florida CRE snapshot:**

1. **Population growth: 1,500+ new residents per week.** The Orlando MSA added over 80,000 people last year — that's demand for housing, retail, medical, and services that directly drives commercial real estate fundamentals.

2. **Industrial vacancy: under 5%.** Warehouse and logistics space across Orange, Seminole, and Osceola counties is being absorbed almost as fast as it's built. E-commerce fulfillment and the tourism supply chain are the primary drivers. Cap rates for Class A industrial are sitting at 5.25%–6.50%.

3. **NNN cap rates are compressing.** National credit tenants (Dollar General, Walgreens, Starbucks) in growth corridors are trading at 4.75%–6.25%. Twelve months ago, the same deals were 25–50 bps wider. Institutional capital is flowing in.

4. **75 million+ annual visitors.** This isn't just a tourism stat — it's the engine behind Orlando's retail, hospitality, and service-sector commercial demand. Epic Universe opens in 2025, adding fuel.

**What it means for investors:**
If you're evaluating Central Florida, the fundamentals are as strong as they've been in a decade. The question isn't whether to invest here — it's which submarket and asset class gives you the best risk-adjusted return for your capital.
