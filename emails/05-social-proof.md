I work with a lot of out-of-state investors. The pattern is almost always the same:

They start looking at Florida because of one thing — **no state income tax.** Then they stay because the fundamentals are genuinely strong.

**The tax math is hard to ignore.**

An investor earning $500K in annual income (including rental income and capital gains) saves roughly:

- **$52,500/year** vs. California (13.3% top rate)
- **$44,000/year** vs. New York (10.9% top rate + NYC tax)
- **$35,000/year** vs. New Jersey (10.75% top rate)
- **$24,750/year** vs. Illinois (4.95% flat rate)

Over a 10-year hold period on a commercial property, that's $250K–$500K+ in tax savings alone. And that's before you factor in property-level income.

**But tax savings aren't the whole story.**

The investors who keep coming back — and building portfolios here — do so because Central Florida checks every box:

- **Population growth:** Orlando MSA is adding 1,500+ people per week. That's demand for every asset class — retail, medical, industrial, multifamily.
- **Employment diversification:** This isn't a one-industry market. Defense, aerospace, healthcare, tech, tourism, and logistics all contribute. Major employers include Lockheed Martin, Siemens Energy, AdventHealth, and the University of Central Florida.
- **Institutional capital validation:** Blackstone, KKR, and Prologis have all made significant Central Florida acquisitions. When institutional money moves in, it signals long-term confidence.

**Two recent client examples:**

A Bay Area investor sold a 4-unit residential building in Oakland and 1031-exchanged into a Dollar General NNN in Lake County. Result: 6.1% cap (vs. 3.8% on the Oakland property), zero management burden, 15-year lease, and eliminated $47K/year in California state tax.

A New Jersey-based physician group acquired a medical office building near Lake Nona Medical City for $3.2M. They're now earning an 7.4% cash-on-cash return with a 10-year NNN lease to a multi-location dental practice — and paying zero state income tax on the income.

**The question I always get: "Why Central Florida instead of Miami or Tampa?"**

Miami trades at 50–100+ bps tighter cap rates with higher insurance costs. Tampa has compressed significantly and is less affordable for entry-level investors. Central Florida — particularly the Orlando MSA, Brevard County, and Lake County — still offers room to run with lower price-per-foot and stronger growth trajectories.
